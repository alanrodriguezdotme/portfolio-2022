Modern Design

This year, we're starting a new conversation about design language.

We have a point of view.

And we have lots of things to talk about

Our product offerings (and how we deliver them) continue to increase-so, too, does our brand development, brand identity and personality, design patterns, platform influences. Given the broad range of devices, operating systems, services and modalities, this gets (even) more challenging.

Brand and experience hand in hand

All good brands have a recognizable, over-arching aesthetic, a brand voice, and a distinct look and feel.

The end result should be clear, simple and intuitive.

Less is more, especially in a company with vast opportunities to do everything, for everyone.
As One Microsoft, our focus is on integration where there's significant value-add, and the potential to greatly enhance our products or experience. If we follow this strategy, determining what brand a product or experience rolls up to should be easy, especially in our new simplified brand architecture.

Especially, when you think about these

Hero apps & experiences

These are product experiences that are core to the Microsoft hub, such as Outlook Mail, Calendar, Powerpoint, Excel, Word, etc.

Snack apps & experiences

These are tailored, mobile, "bite sized" product experiences that have specific focus areas, attract new users, and may pull from, and/or push to the hero/hub experiences.

First Party

These are hero, or snack apps and experiences that designed for our own windows platform and devices.

Third Party

These are hero, or snack apps and experiences that designed for other platforms, outside of Windows, such as iOS, or Android.

App first, or platform first?

It's more of a harmonious, and somewhat symbiotic relationship that empowers users, and elevates their experience on every platform.

We think it's a bit of both.

Leveraging this language, design guidance, assets and tools should help determine the right balance of brand identity, personality, core UI and platform influences, and directly influence the design direction of our products.

Our apps, experiences and services should feel like our apps, experiences and services, on all platforms, but be familiar, intuitive, and leverage the platform in ways the user would expect.
