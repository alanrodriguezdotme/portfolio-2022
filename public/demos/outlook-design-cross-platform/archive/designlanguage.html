Design Language

The elements of a brand (such as colors, typography, layout, scale, composition, iconography, motion, logos, product name, voice) that together, compositionally, identifies and distinguishes the brand.

Our brand identity comes from our design language, and is our personality.

These elements come together through a systematic design approach that becomes recognizable, builds familiarity, trust, and brand equity through our user experience.

Brand Identity

Our product is our brand, and our brand should resonate through both the experience and content at every level.

What brand or category does a specific product or experience roll up to? Is there an inherent relationship to existing brands in our ecosystem?
Should the product have an explicit logo mark, or logotype such as Microsoft, Office, Windows, Xbox, Skype, etc.?

Personality

What should the personality of our product, apps, and/or experiences be?

The personality of our products should be unapologetically Microsoft, recognizable as part of our product ecosystem.
We're confident in the Microsoft Design Language, in its ability to connect our products and services aesthetically, and reinforce our One Microsoft strategy.
We are also confident in its ability to adapt to a wide range of products and experiences. It's an extensible language that we've seen come to life in communication experiences, productivity applications, in both work and life, from Windows, to Office, to entertainment platforms like Xbox.
These elements come together through a systematic design approach that becomes recognizable, builds familiarity, trust, and brand equity through our user experience.

Cross Platform Personality

We should celebrate our established content first aesthetic more than ever, on all platforms, and leave the 'chrome' to the platform.

We should strive to achieve the right balance of our Microsoft brand identity, or personality, on other non-Microsoft platforms. To do so, we have to take into account brand, brands identity & personality, design patterns, and the platform.

The goal should be to feel recognizable as part of our ecosystem, yet appropriate and familiar to the respective platform.

Our products should 'look' like Microsoft through our visual design language, or personality, yet 'feel' appropriate and familiar to the platform, or vehicle in which we deliver them.

Snack Apps Personality

Intend to be different based on strategy

Exceptions to this recommendation might be in areas where we have very explicit goals, or intend to be different based on strategy, and therefore need to break the paradigm to meet the specific experience, business, or strategic goals.
An example of this might be our 'Snack Apps' initiative, where we are investing in a focused set of single-purpose apps, which are tailored experiences, targeting a specific demographic, or space, and therefore require deviation in personality, or style.
