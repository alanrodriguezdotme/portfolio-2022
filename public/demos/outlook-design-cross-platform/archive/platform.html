Platform Patterns

This area covers a wide range of common solutions, or best practices, to interaction design problems within a specific context. Patterns are existing, and often documented UI interactions and flows.

Patterns form experiential mental models that make it easier for users to understand the interface, or experience, and accomplish their tasks.

Most established platforms have a framework- level, systematic approach to the way a user interacts with the device, or UI.

Through these common interactions, users learn how the system works, and often unknowingly build 'mental models', or a sense of how something works. Learn it here, apply it there, and likely get an expected outcome. Simply put, this builds trust through perception in that there's a predicted outcome from an untested input. Mental models are in some ways a form of subconscious reasoning, ie, people are unaware until asked to observe them - a form of intuition.
This is the sweet spot, where we find the balance between "unapologetically Microsoft" via personality, and familiarity of the platform paradigms. By leveraging existing user expectations, behaviors, paradigms, patterns, and mental models, per the respective platform and bringing our signature content first aesthetic, personality, or Microsoft design language, we find harmony, and build a cross-platform ecosystem.
